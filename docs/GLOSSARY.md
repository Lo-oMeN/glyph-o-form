# Glyph-o-betics Glossary
## Complete Terminology Reference

**Version:** 1.0  
**Purpose:** Define every term used in the Looman-GCE ecosystem

---

## A

### Absence (∅)
One of the four atoms. Represented by the symbol `∅` (empty set). Absence is not mere emptiness but **potential space**—the kenotic void from which manifestation emerges. In 7-segment glyphs, absence creates breathing room and allows other atoms to resonate more strongly.

**Related:** Kenosis, Void, Potential

### Alphabet Helix
Layer 7 of the Looman-GCE architecture. The user-facing spiral interface that allows navigation through the glyph constellation. Visualizes the relationship between letters, glyphs, and semantic fields in a three-dimensional helix structure.

**Related:** Layer Model, Constellation, GCE

### ASCII Glyph
Text-based representation of a glyph using standard characters:
```
      ￿
   ￿     ￿
      —
   ∅     ∅
      —
```

**Related:** Visualization, Rendering

### Atom
The fundamental building blocks of glyphs. Four atoms exist: Point (·), Line (—), Curve (￿), and Absence (∅). All language is decomposed into these geometric primitives.

**Related:** Four Atoms, Glyph, Segment

## B

### Bhakti-Kenosis Engine
Layer 6 of the Looman-GCE architecture. The ethical core that ensures all glyph operations align with kenotic (self-emptying) principles. Prevents coercive or extractive applications of the technology.

**Related:** Kenosis, Chiral-Guardian, Ethics

### Bulletproof Validation
A comprehensive stress-testing framework based on knowledge graph principles. Ensures the Looman-GCE architecture is resilient against technical, social, and adversarial attacks.

**Related:** Security, Anti-fragility

## C

### Chiral-Guardian
The ethical filter within the Glyph-o-betics system. Uses chirality (handedness/asymmetry) to distinguish kenotic (generous, emptying) from non-kenotic (extractive, coercive) applications. **The ESA (Ethical-Security-Aesthetic) filter.**

**Related:** Bhakti-Kenosis Engine, Ethics, Kenosis

### Compression Ratio
The efficiency gain from encoding text as glyphs. Typically **25-40% smaller** than UTF-8 text while preserving semantic relationships and adding navigational metadata.

**Related:** .gpkg Format, Encoding

### Connection Line
Visual lines drawn between glyphs in a constellation that share high resonance (>0.3). Thickness indicates resonance strength: faint (>0.3), bold (>0.6), glowing (>0.9).

**Related:** Resonance, Constellation, Möbius Lock

### Constellation
A network of multiple glyphs arranged in geometric space with connection lines showing semantic resonance. Sentences, paragraphs, and documents become constellations you can navigate.

**Related:** Word, Sentence, Connection Line, Resonance

### Curve (￿)
One of the four atoms. Represented by the symbol `￿`. Signifies transformation, openness, fluidity, and continuous change. Higher "energy" than line in atom dominance hierarchy.

**Related:** Four Atoms, Flow, Transformation

## D

### Decompression
The process of expanding a compressed glyph (3 bytes) back into its full representation including ASCII art, SVG, semantic description, and English approximation.

**Related:** Compression, .gpkg Format

### Descent (Pathway)
One of three parallel processes that transform words into glyphs:
- **Phonetic Descent:** Sound patterns → curves and flows
- **Orthographic Descent:** Letter shapes → strokes and angles  
- **Semantic Descent:** Meaning → topological structure

**Related:** Three Descents, Fusion

### Dimi-Phor
Layer 1 of the Looman-GCE architecture. The phonetic-to-geometric mapping engine that transforms spoken/written sounds into glyph atoms. "Dimensional phonetic resolver."

**Related:** Layer Model, Phonetics

### Drag Operation
User interaction where glyph elements are clicked and moved. In GeoGebra MVP, dragging updates the glyph in real-time with 60 FPS cascading updates.

**Related:** Interaction, GeoGebra, UI

---

## E

### Edge Node
Distributed computing node in the Glyph-o-betics network. Runs the Looman-GCE runtime locally, enabling offline operation and mesh synchronization.

**Related:** Deployment, Mesh, Network

### Emergent Property
Characteristics that arise from the interaction of glyph atoms that cannot be predicted from the atoms alone. Resonance between glyphs is emergent.

**Related:** Resonance, Complexity

### Encoding
The binary representation of glyphs. Each glyph compresses to **3 bytes** with atom data (2 bits per atom × 7 atoms = 14 bits) plus flags (orientation, polarity, lock status).

**Related:** Compression, .gpkg Format

### ESA Filter
**Ethical-Security-Aesthetic** filter. The three-fold validation applied to all glyph operations: Is it ethical (kenotic)? Is it secure (non-exploitable)? Is it beautiful (harmonious)?

**Related:** Chiral-Guardian, Ethics, Validation

**Related:** Community, Governance

### Four Atoms
The complete set of geometric primitives: Point (·), Line (—), Curve (￿), Absence (∅). All language decomposes into these.

**Related:** Atom, Glyph

### Free-Energy Descent
Layer 5 of the Looman-GCE architecture. The control law that governs how glyphs optimize: `u = −K∇ℱ(u)`, where ℱ is free energy combining structural coherence and kenotic reward.

**Related:** Layer Model, Optimization, Control Theory

### Fusion
The process of combining three descent pathways (phonetic, orthographic, semantic) into a single glyph representation using majority voting with kenotic bias.

**Related:** Three Descents, Kenotic Bias

## G

### GCE
**Glyph Constellation Engine**. The complete system for transforming language into geometric glyphs and managing their relationships.

**Related:** Looman-GCE, Constellation

### Geometric VM
Layer 0 of the Looman-GCE architecture. The virtual machine that executes geometric operations. In the MVP, implemented as GeoGebra with 60 FPS cascading updates.

**Related:** Layer Model, GeoGebra, VM

### GeoGebra
Open-source dynamic mathematics software used as the Layer 0 implementation. Provides draggable, interactive glyph visualization.

**Related:** Geometric VM, Implementation

### Glyph
A single word transformed into a 7-segment geometric representation using the four atoms. The fundamental unit of Glyph-o-betics.

**Related:** Word, Atom, 7-Segment

### Glyph-o-betics
The complete framework for cross-linguistic semantic visualization using geometric primitives. From "glyph" + "alphabetics."

**Related:** Looman-GCE, Universal Language

### Glyph String
The compact text representation of a glyph's atoms: e.g., `￿￿￿——∅∅` for LOVE. Seven characters representing the seven segments.

**Related:** Glyph, Encoding, Visualization

### Golden Harmonic (Φ)
The golden ratio (≈1.618) used as a weighting factor in resonance calculations and kenotic optimization. Creates aesthetically harmonious relationships.

**Related:** Phi, Resonance, Beauty

### .gpkg Format
**Glyph Package**. The file format for compressed constellations. Contains: header, glyph array (3 bytes each), connection matrix, metadata.

**Related:** Compression, Constellation, Encoding

### Letter-to-Atom Mapping
The table defining which atoms each Latin letter decomposes into. A becomes [·, —], B becomes [￿, —], etc.

**Related:** Orthographic Descent, Atom

### Line (—)
One of the four atoms. Represented by the symbol `—`. Signifies connection, structure, direction, and assertion. Medium "energy" in atom dominance hierarchy.

**Related:** Four Atoms, Structure, Connection

### Lock Status
A flag in the compressed glyph format indicating whether the glyph has achieved **Möbius lock**—topological persistence that prevents further change.

**Related:** Möbius Lock, Persistence, Compression

### Looman-GCE
**LOOman Geometric Constellation Engine**. The complete technical implementation including all 8 layers. Named after its creator's handle.

**Related:** GCE, Layer Model, Architecture

### LOVE Glyph

In TDA (Topological Data Analysis), features that survive across multiple scales. A glyph achieves "lock" when its topology is persistent.

**Related:** TDA, Möbius Lock, Topology

### Phi (Φ)
The golden ratio, approximately 1.618. Used throughout the system for resonance weighting, animation timing, and harmonic proportions.

Layer 4 of the Looman-GCE architecture. Uses Topological Data Analysis (TDA) to identify persistent features in glyph constellations.

**Related:** Layer Model, TDA, Persistence

### Phonetic Descent
One of the three descent pathways. Maps speech sounds to atoms: vowels → curves, plosives → points, fricatives → lines.

### Point (·)
One of the four atoms. Represented by the symbol `·`. Signifies focus, assertion, position, and discrete presence. Lowest "energy" in atom dominance hierarchy.

### Potential
The state of being possible but not yet actualized. In RSTL logic: `△ POTENTIAL` between `∅ NULL` and `■ ACTUALIZED`.

**Related:** RSTL, Absence, Kenosis

## R

### Rendering
The visual output of a glyph: ASCII art, SVG, PNG, or interactive GeoGebra display. Multiple render formats supported.

**Related:** Visualization, ASCII Glyph, SVG

### Resonance
The semantic similarity between two glyphs, measured as a score from 0 to 1. High resonance (>0.3) creates connection lines in constellations.

**Formula:** Resonance = cosine_similarity + absence_bonus + curve_flow + golden_harmonic

**Related:** Connection Line, Constellation, Similarity

### Resonance Matrix
A table showing resonance scores between multiple glyphs. Visualizes the semantic relationship network.

**Related:** Resonance, Constellation, Visualization

### RSTL
**Real-Symbolic-Transcendent Logic**. Layer 2 of the Looman-GCE architecture. A trinary logic system with states: ∅ NULL, △ POTENTIAL, ■ ACTUALIZED.

**Related:** Layer Model, Logic, Trinary

## S

### Segment
One of seven positions in the glyph lattice: a, b, c, d, e, f, g. Each holds one atom.

```
      a (top)
   f     b
      g (middle)
   e     c
      d (bottom)
```

**Related:** 7-Segment, Atom, Lattice

### Segment Bias
The tendency of certain letters to map to specific segments based on their stroke patterns. L (vertical) → f or b segments.

**Related:** Orthographic Descent, Mapping

### Semantic Descent
One of the three descent pathways. Maps word meaning to topological structure using word embeddings and semantic fields.

**Related:** Three Descents, Meaning, Vector Space

### Shared Edges
When letters stack in a word, their touching edges fuse. L's bottom becomes O's top, creating seamless vertical integration.

**Related:** Stacking, Fusion, Compression

### 7-Segment Lattice
The standard topology for all glyphs. Seven positions arranged like a digital display, holding atoms that compose the geometric form.

**Related:** Segment, Lattice, Topology

### Sovereign
Self-governing; not controlled by external authorities. Glyph-o-betics is sovereign technology—open source, distributed, kenotic.

**Related:** Ethics, Open Source, Distribution

### Stacking
The vertical arrangement of letters in a word. Letters don't sequence left-to-right; they **stack and fuse** into a single glyph.

**Related:** Compression, Shared Edges, Fusion

**Topological Data Analysis**. Mathematical framework for identifying persistent features in complex data. Used in Layer 4 (Phi-Crystal).

**Related:** Phi-Crystal, Persistence, Topology

### Three Descents
The three parallel pathways (phonetic, orthographic, semantic) that transform words into glyphs, fused into the final representation.

**Related:** Descent, Fusion, Phonetic, Orthographic, Semantic

### Thundering Grace Engine (TGE)
The poetic name for the complete Looman-GCE implementation. "Thundering" = power; "Grace" = kenotic beauty.

**Related:** Looman-GCE, Layer Model

### Topology
The study of properties preserved under continuous deformation. Glyph constellations have topological properties (connectedness, holes, persistence).

**Related:** TDA, Persistence, Structure

### Trinary Logic
Logic with three states instead of binary true/false. RSTL uses: ∅ NULL (no), △ POTENTIAL (maybe), ■ ACTUALIZED (yes).

**Related:** RSTL, Logic, Potential
